What Social Media Is For (And What It’s Not)
Social Media Is For:

Reminding people you exist

Showing the restaurant is active and alive

Reinforcing food quality and atmosphere

Highlighting events, specials, and moments

Social Media Is Not For:

Solving service issues

Long explanations or arguments

Daily overthinking

Competing with influencers

If it feels like work, the system is wrong.

Platform Focus (Restaurants Only)

Do not try to be everywhere.

Primary Platforms

Instagram – Visual proof, stories, reels

Facebook – Local audience, events, updates

Secondary (Optional)

Google Business Profile – Updates, photos, credibility

TikTok – Only if you already have momentum

Ignore platforms that don’t move local traffic.

Posting Frequency (Minimum Effective Dose)
Recommended Baseline

3–4 posts per week (total)

Stories: 3–5 short clips per week

Reels / short video: 1–2 per week

More than this is optional — not required.

Silence kills reach faster than imperfect posts.

Best Times to Post (Restaurant-Tested)

These windows align with guest behavior, not algorithms.

Weekdays

10:30 AM – 11:30 AM (lunch decision window)

4:30 PM – 5:30 PM (dinner planning window)

Weekends

9:00 AM – 10:30 AM (brunch / day plans)

4:00 PM – 5:00 PM (dinner plans)

Posting outside these windows lowers impact.

Content Buckets (Rotate, Don’t Reinvent)

You only need 5 repeatable categories.

1. Food Proof

Plates leaving the kitchen

Close-ups, no filters

Steam, texture, real light

Rule: If it doesn’t make you hungry, don’t post it.

2. Restaurant Life

Busy dining room

Bar energy

Music nights, events

Guests want proof the place has energy.

3. People

Staff highlights

Team moments

Behind-the-scenes prep

Human faces build trust faster than logos.

4. Specials & Events

Limited-time items

Live music

Seasonal promotions

Scarcity drives action.

5. Brand Signals

Values

Hospitality moments

Community ties

This builds memory, not clicks.

Caption Rules (Keep It Simple)

1–3 short sentences

No essays

No hashtags overload (3–5 max)

Location always implied or stated

Good captions feel like a human talking, not marketing copy.

Stories (The Most Underrated Tool)

Stories are:

Low effort

High trust

Forgiving

Use them for:

Prep shots

Night energy

Quick updates

Stories do not need polish — they need honesty.

Engagement Rules (Important)
Always:

Respond to comments

Like tagged posts

Acknowledge mentions

Never:

Argue publicly

Defend mistakes

Over-explain

Take issues offline immediately.

Reviews & Social (Connected, Not Separate)

Thank positive reviewers publicly

Respond calmly and professionally to negatives

Never restate menu items in responses

Invite private follow-up when appropriate

Social media reinforces reputation — it doesn’t replace it.

Owner Time Protection Rules

To avoid burnout:

Batch content once per week

Schedule posts in advance

Delegate posting when possible

Do not scroll during service

If social media pulls you out of the business, it’s misconfigured.

The Standard

Your social presence should feel:

Active

Welcoming

Reliable

Real

Not trendy.
Not perfect.
Not exhausting.

Bottom Line

Post enough to be remembered.
Not so much that it becomes your job.
