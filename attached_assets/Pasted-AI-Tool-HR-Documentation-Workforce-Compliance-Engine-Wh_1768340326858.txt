AI Tool: HR Documentation & Workforce Compliance Engine
What It Does

This AI tool converts workplace issues into clear, factual, and State Workforce Commission–compliant HR documentation designed to hold up under unemployment claims, audits, and disputes.

Instead of informal notes, emotional write-ups, or inconsistent discipline, the tool produces objective, policy-based counseling forms and warnings that align with state workforce commission standards for due process, consistency, and documentation.

Core Function

Identify the issue → apply the correct disciplinary standard → generate compliant documentation that protects the business.

This tool is built to answer one question clearly:

“If this goes to the Workforce Commission, will this document stand?”

How It Works
1. Issue Intake (Plain Language)

The manager explains what happened:

Attendance or tardiness

No-call / no-show

Performance failure

Conduct or policy violation

Guest-related incident

Safety or compliance issue

No legal wording required from the manager.

2. Compliance-Driven Clarification

The AI asks only the questions the Workforce Commission cares about:

What specific policy or expectation was violated?

Was the employee aware of this policy?

Is this a first occurrence or part of a pattern?

Was prior coaching or discipline issued?

Did the behavior impact operations, guests, safety, or coworkers?

Were mitigating circumstances considered?

This ensures procedural fairness and consistency.

3. Severity & Escalation Logic (SWC-Aligned)

The AI determines the appropriate response:

Documented coaching

Written warning

Final written warning

Performance improvement notice

Termination documentation

Incident report (non-disciplinary)

Escalation is based on documented pattern, not emotion.

What It Produces (Compliance-Ready)

The output is a state-compliant HR document that includes:

Objective statement of facts (who, what, when, where — no opinions)

Policy or standard reference (clearly stated expectation)

Employee acknowledgment of expectations

Corrective action required

Opportunity to correct behavior

Clear consequences for failure to improve

At-will employment language (where applicable)

Employee and manager acknowledgment sections

All language is written to:

Avoid retaliation or discriminatory phrasing

Demonstrate fairness and consistency

Support denial of unemployment benefits when appropriate

Workforce Commission Compliance Safeguards

This tool is designed around the principles used by Workforce Commissions when evaluating claims:

✔ Clear policy communication
✔ Prior notice and opportunity to improve
✔ Progressive discipline where appropriate
✔ Consistent enforcement across employees
✔ Objective, documented facts
✔ Signed acknowledgments

Coaching without documentation is invisible to the Workforce Commission.
Documentation without clarity is useless.

This tool ensures neither happens.
