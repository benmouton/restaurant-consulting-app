. Here's what makes Financial Insights the most strategically important upgrade in the whole series — and why a few of these features are particularly critical:
Apple Vision text extraction is listed first for a reason. This is the one module where the current implementation might be silently broken for a large percentage of uploads. Restaurant operators don't export clean text PDFs — they photograph their P&L printouts, screenshot their Toast reports, or download scanned documents from their accountant. Standard PDF parsing returns empty string on all of those. Apple's VNRecognizeTextRequest running on-device handles printed financial documents with near-perfect accuracy, costs nothing, and doesn't require a third-party OCR API. Without this, every scanned upload produces a useless analysis. With it, the whole module actually works.
The Full Analysis structured results card is the biggest UX leap. Right now "Run Full Analysis" streams a wall of text into the chat. After this upgrade it renders as a Financial Health Score donut (operators immediately understand "74/100" — they don't need to read 800 words to know where they stand) + Top 3 Alerts in red callouts + Top 3 Wins in green + a tappable Action Items checklist with a "Save as Playbook" button. That's a complete financial debrief in one scannable view.
Context-aware question chips that change based on what's uploaded are a significant usability upgrade — asking "What's my labor %?" when a Labor Report is uploaded vs. asking general industry questions when nothing is uploaded. Currently all 3 chips are static regardless of context.
The "Save as Playbook" → Living Playbooks cross-module link and the "Ask the Consultant about this" deep link are what make the platform feel like one system instead of isolated tools. An operator runs financial analysis, sees their top 3 issues, taps one button, and lands in Ask the Consultant with those issues already in the input. That's the "Systems That Work" promise made real.
