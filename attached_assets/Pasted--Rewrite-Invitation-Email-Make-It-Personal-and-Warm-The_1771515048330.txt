# Rewrite Invitation Email — Make It Personal and Warm

The current invitation email reads like a generic SaaS onboarding message. These invitations are being sent to trusted friends and valued restaurant operators — people Ben knows personally. The email needs to feel like a personal invitation from a fellow operator, not a corporate welcome email.

---

## Current Problems:
1. "Ben Mouton has given you access to The Restaurant Consultant" — sounds like a system notification, not a personal invitation
2. The feature list is generic bullet points — doesn't convey why this person specifically should care
3. "Your access expires in 7 days" — feels transactional and pressuring
4. The overall tone is cold and corporate

---

## Rewrite the Email

### Header area:
- Keep "The Restaurant CONSULTANT" branding at the top
- Add the chef hat logo icon if possible

### Email body — rewrite to this tone and structure:

**Subject line options (pick one):**
- "Ben built something for you"
- "An operator's toolbox — Ben wants you to try it"
- "Ben Mouton invited you to The Restaurant Consultant"

**Body:**

```
Hey [First Name],

It's Ben. I've been building something I think you're going to find useful.

The Restaurant Consultant is an operations platform I built for people like us — 
independent operators who are tired of carrying everything themselves. It's got 
the systems, frameworks, and tools I wish I had when I was figuring things out 
the hard way.

Here's what's inside:

→ Kitchen readiness scoring — know if you're ready before service, not during it
→ HR documentation that actually holds up at a hearing  
→ Training programs your new hires can follow without you hovering
→ A staffing engine that tells you exactly who to cut and when
→ Crisis playbooks for the nights that go sideways
→ An operations consultant you can ask anything, anytime

I'm giving you full access for 7 days to explore everything — no credit card, 
no strings. I'd genuinely love your feedback on it.

[GET STARTED →]

If you have questions or want me to walk you through it, just text me. 
You know how to reach me.

— Ben
```

### Footer:
```
The Restaurant Consultant
Systems that work on your worst night.

Austin, TX

You received this because Ben Mouton personally invited you to try the platform.
Don't want these emails? Reply with "unsubscribe" and we'll stop.
```

---

## Key Changes:
1. **First person voice** — "It's Ben. I've been building something..." not "Ben Mouton has given you access to..."
2. **Benefits, not features** — "Know if you're ready before service, not during it" instead of "12 operational domains"
3. **Personal close** — "just text me. You know how to reach me." reinforces this is a personal invite from a friend
4. **Softer urgency** — "full access for 7 days to explore everything" feels generous, not pressuring
5. **Asks for feedback** — "I'd genuinely love your feedback" makes the recipient feel valued, not sold to

---

## Technical Implementation:
- The email template is likely in your server-side code — search for the invitation email template (could be in `/server/` or wherever email sending is handled)
- Replace `[First Name]` with the recipient's first name if available, otherwise use the part of their email before the @ symbol
- The sender name should show as "Ben Mouton" or "Ben from The Restaurant Consultant" — not just "The Restaurant Consultant"
- Make sure the "Get Started" button is prominent — keep the cyan color, it works

---

## Also Update:
- **The re-invitation email** (for expired invites that get resent) — should say something like "Hey [Name], just wanted to make sure you saw this — your last invite expired but I've sent you a fresh one. No rush, but I think you'll dig it."
- **Any follow-up reminder emails** — keep the same warm, personal tone throughout the sequence
