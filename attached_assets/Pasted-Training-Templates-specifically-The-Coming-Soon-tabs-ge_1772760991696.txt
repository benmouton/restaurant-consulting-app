Training Templates specifically:
The Coming Soon tabs get genuinely useful instead of dead ends. Right now Host, Bartender, Manager, and Busser are empty placeholders that tell an operator to go use a different tab. After this upgrade, each one has an AI outline generator — an operator types "greet guests, manage waitlist, seat parties" and gets a structured 5-day Host training outline in seconds. That's a real deliverable they can print and use today, before the full manual is ever built. It turns 4 useless screens into 4 working tools immediately.
Badge color-coding on the TOC is a small change with big usability impact. Right now every section item looks identical — overview, procedure, and policy badges are all the same. Color-coding them (gold = overview, blue = procedure, amber = policy, green = quiz) means an operator scanning the manual can immediately find "show me all the procedures" without reading every label.
"Customize with AI" on every section is the highest-value new feature. Operators are trained that these manuals are for Mouton's Bistro & Bar — but the Day 3 beverage content doesn't know about their specific cocktail menu or their house rules. The Customize button lets them type "add a section about our signature po'boy modifications" and get it woven into the section. Combined with "Add as Notes" vs "Replace Section," operators can either annotate or fully personalize their manuals. That's the difference between a template and their training system.
The Handbook Preview getting a gold border + Download PDF closes the biggest gap in that tab — operators build the handbook but there's no clean way to deliver it to a new hire. The framed white preview card inside the dark UI, with a one-tap Download as PDF, makes the whole Handbook Builder feel finished.
Active assignment cards with day-by-day progress checkboxes turn "Assign Training" from a button that does nothing visible into an actual tracking tool. An operator assigns a new server on Monday, checks off Day 1 Tuesday, and can see at a glance where every new hire is in their training week.
