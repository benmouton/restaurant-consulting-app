# Page-Specific Improvements — Skills Certification Engine

This is one of the most differentiated features in your app. Most restaurant tech handles scheduling and POS — almost nobody offers behavior-based certification with customizable rubrics. The Standards tab is particularly impressive. Here's how to make it even stronger.

---

## AI Branding Fix

**Screenshot 1:** The warning banner says "Go to the Standards tab to set up your restaurant's standards. Without them, the AI will use generic industry defaults."
- Change to: **"Go to the Standards tab to set up your restaurant's standards. Without them, generic industry defaults will be used."**

---

## 1. Certify Tab Improvements

### Add context about what the certification scenario will look like
- Right now the form is: Trainee Name, Role, Phase, then "Generate Certification Scenario." An operator doesn't know what they're about to get.
- Add a brief description below each Certification Phase option:
  - **Shadow Phase:** "Knowledge + judgment scenarios. Lower difficulty, more guidance." ✅ (already there — good)
  - **Perform Phase:** "Real-world scenarios the trainee must handle independently. Moderate difficulty."
  - **Certify Phase:** "Full service simulation. High difficulty, no hints. Pass/fail."
- Below the Generate button, add: "You'll receive a scenario your trainee responds to verbally or in writing, scored against your restaurant's standards."

### Show a sample scenario
- Add a collapsed "See example scenario →" section below the button
- Show a brief example of what a generated certification scenario looks like so the trainer knows what to expect before generating one
- This is especially important for first-time users who've never seen the output

### Connect to employee records
- The "Trainee Name" field should be a **searchable dropdown of employees from the Staff tab** in Scheduling, not a free text field
- This ensures certification results are tied to a specific employee record and avoids typos or duplicate names
- If the staff list isn't populated yet, fall back to free text with a note: "Add employees in Staff Scheduling to auto-populate this list"

---

## 2. Dashboard Tab Improvements

### The empty state needs more than "No certification tests yet"
- Replace with a more informative empty state:
  - Show a brief visual of what the dashboard will look like once populated (a mockup or wireframe)
  - Add text: "Once you run certification tests, you'll see: pass/fail rates by role, individual employee scores, and certification expiration tracking."
  - Add a CTA button: "Run Your First Certification →" that takes them to the Certify tab

### When populated, the dashboard should show:
- **Team certification overview:**
  - Total employees certified vs uncertified
  - Breakdown by role: "Servers: 4/6 certified, Kitchen: 3/5 certified"
  - Progress bar per role showing certification coverage
- **Recent attempts table:**
  - Employee name, role, phase, score, pass/fail, date
  - Click to view full scenario and scoring breakdown
- **Expiration tracking:**
  - If certifications expire (e.g., every 6 months), show who's due for recertification
  - "3 certifications expiring this month" with a list
- **Trends:**
  - Average scores over time (are new hires getting better?)
  - Most common failure areas (helps identify training gaps)

## 3. Standards Tab Improvements

### The Standards tab is the strongest part — protect and enhance it

The content you've filled in is excellent:
- Service Philosophy: "Warm, fast, professional service. Never argue with guests."
- Steps of Service with specific timing targets
- Speed Targets (Drinks 3 min, Apps 8 min, Entrees 15 min)
- ROAR recovery framework
- Alcohol policy with ID rules, max drinks, cut-off rules
- Safety & Sanitation rules
- Critical Errors (Auto-Fail) list
- Scoring Rubric Weights with configurable pass threshold

This is genuinely comprehensive. A few enhancements:

### Add a "Standards Completion" indicator
- Show a progress bar or checklist at the top of the Standards tab:
  - ✅ Service Philosophy
  - ✅ Steps of Service
  - ✅ Speed Targets
  - ✅ Recovery Framework
  - ✅ Alcohol Policy
  - ✅ Safety & Sanitation
  - ✅ Critical Errors
  - ✅ Scoring Rubric
- This helps operators see at a glance if they've missed any sections
- Show "Standards complete ✓" or "7 of 8 sections configured" at the top

### Make the "Load Default Template" button more prominent on first visit
- When no standards are configured (screenshot 1 shows the warning), the Standards tab should lead with the default template button front and center, not as a small button in the corner
- Frame it as: "Start with industry-standard defaults and customize to your restaurant" with a large CTA
- After loading, show what was loaded so the operator can review and edit

### Add section-level validation
- For Steps of Service: if Greeting Timing is left blank, show a subtle warning
- For Speed Targets: if any are blank, flag it
- For Scoring Rubric: validate that weights total 100 (you already show "100/100" — good) and show an error state in red if they don't
- For Critical Errors: suggest a minimum of 3 items — if fewer, show: "Consider adding more critical errors. Most restaurants have 5-8."

### Add a "Preview as Rubric" button
- Let the operator see their standards formatted as the actual scoring rubric that will be used during certification
- Show it as a clean table:
  - Category | Weight | What's Being Evaluated
  - Prioritization & Triage (25%) | How well did the trainee identify and prioritize the issue?
  - Guest Communication (25%) | Did the trainee communicate effectively with the guest?
  - etc.
- This helps operators understand exactly how their standards translate to scoring

### Connect standards to other parts of the app
- The Speed Targets (Drinks 3 min, Apps 8 min, Entrees 15 min) should also feed into the Kitchen Operations page's ticket time tracking
- The Recovery Framework (ROAR) should be referenced in the Service Standards domain's Guest Recovery Decision Advisor
- The Alcohol Policy should appear in the Training Manual's relevant day
- This creates a **single source of truth** — operators define standards once and they flow everywhere

---

## 4. Certification Report / Scorecard

### After a certification is scored, generate a printable report
- The report should include:
  - Employee name, role, date, phase tested
  - Scenario description
  - Employee's response (or trainer's notes on the response)
  - Score by category with rubric weights applied
  - Overall score and pass/fail
  - Areas of strength
  - Areas for improvement with specific retraining recommendations
  - If failed: "Recommended retest date" and "Focus areas before retest"
  - Trainer signature line and employee signature line
- Add "Print," "Export PDF," and "Save to Employee Record" buttons
- This scorecard becomes part of the employee's file — critical for both development and legal protection

---

## 5. Multi-Role Standards

### Allow different standards per role
- A server's standards are different from a cook's standards
- Consider allowing operators to create multiple standards sets:
  - "Server Standards" (front-of-house focused)
  - "Kitchen Standards" (food safety, speed, station management focused)
  - "Bartender Standards" (alcohol policy heavy, drink knowledge)
  - "Manager Standards" (leadership, crisis response, P&L awareness)
- When running a certification, auto-load the relevant standards based on the role selected
- This is a bigger feature but would make the certification system truly comprehensive
