Principle

Core Principle: The Kitchen Is a Production System

The kitchen exists to produce consistent food at speed, under pressure, every shift.

It is not:
	•	a platform for ego
	•	a democracy during service
	•	a place where talent overrides standards

When personalities drive the kitchen, consistency dies.
When systems drive the kitchen, food quality becomes predictable.

If results change based on who’s on the line, the system is broken.

⸻

Checklist

Prep Discipline Standards (Non-Negotiable)

DAILY PREP REQUIREMENTS

□ Par levels reviewed against reservations + historical walk-ins
□ Prep list written by KM or Sous before prep cook arrives
□ FIFO rotation verified during prep, not after
□ All containers dated, labeled, and legible
□ Prep completion signed off before service begins

Rule:
If prep is not signed off, it is not complete.
Unfinished prep discovered during service = planning failure.

⸻

WEEKLY CONTROLS

□ Walk-in fully organized and deep-cleaned
□ Par levels adjusted using actual usage, not habit
□ Waste log reviewed with top 3 causes identified
□ Equipment checklist completed and deficiencies logged

Repeating shortages are never a cook problem — they are a system failure.

Framework

Ticket Flow Protocol

Expo Is the Control Tower

EXPO CONTROLS THE WINDOW. PERIOD.

There is one voice at the pass.
Chaos at the window = leadership failure, not effort failure.

⸻

STANDARD FLOW

• Expo reads ticket and assigns stations
• Stations verbally confirm with “Heard”
• Station calls “Walking [item]” when plating
• Expo calls “Pick up [table]” when complete
• Runner confirms table number, not plates

Silent kitchens create slow kitchens.

⸻

TICKET TIMING STANDARDS

• Appetizers: 8–10 minutes
• Entrées: 15–18 minutes after apps clear
• Desserts: 6–8 minutes

Consistency matters more than occasional speed.

⸻

DYING IN THE WINDOW (QUALITY CONTROL RULE)

• Food waiting over 90 seconds = quality failure
• Expo immediately calls for runner
• Third occurrence in one service = runner coaching required

Food dying in the window counts as a BOH miss — not FOH.

⸻

Framework

Line Discipline Rules

• One voice at the window: Expo
• No arguing during service — resolve after
• Missed calls are acknowledged and corrected immediately
• Repeated breakdowns trigger post-shift review

The line is not a debate stage.
Service time is execution time.

⸻

Script

BOH Performance Coaching Script

(Private. Specific. Calm. Documented when patterned.)

“Hey, can we step off the line for a minute?”

“Tonight I noticed [specific behavior] — for example,
‘tickets sat in the window over 90 seconds multiple times.’”

“Help me understand what was happening.”

[Listen fully. Do not interrupt.]

“Here’s what needs to happen going forward:
[clear, observable expectation].”

“Can you commit to that?”

“Thanks. I know it’s busy. Let’s get back in there.”

Document after service if the issue repeats.

⸻

Enforcement & Accountability Loop

(This is what makes the system real.)

Every BOH standard must include:
	1.	Owner (KM / Expo / Runner)
	2.	Expectation (written, observable)
	3.	Verification (when/how it’s checked)
	4.	Correction (real-time)
	5.	Documentation (patterns, not one-offs)

If the same issue repeats:
	•	redesign the system first
	•	retrain second
	•	discipline only when standards are clear and ignored
