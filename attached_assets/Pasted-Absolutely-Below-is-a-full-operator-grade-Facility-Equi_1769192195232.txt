Equipment Risk Classification

(Not All Equipment Is Equal)

Every restaurant must classify equipment into three risk tiers.

Tier 1 – Service-Critical (Failure = Immediate Crisis)

Failure stops service or creates safety risk.

Examples:

Refrigeration

Cooking line (grills, fryers, ranges)

POS / network hardware

Dish machine (in full-service)

HVAC in extreme weather

Rule:
Tier 1 equipment gets scheduled preventative maintenance. No exceptions.

Tier 2 – Service-Impacting (Failure = Degraded Experience)

Failure slows service or increases labor.

Examples:

Prep equipment

Ice machines

Coffee systems

Smallwares critical to volume

Rule:
Tier 2 equipment gets routine inspection and cleaning schedules.

Tier 3 – Non-Critical (Failure = Inconvenience)

Failure does not immediately impact guests.

Examples:

Office equipment

Decorative lighting

Non-essential fixtures

Rule:
Fix when practical. Do not prioritize during service.

The “Fail Loud vs Fail Silent” Rule

Fail Loud Equipment

Breaks obviously

Forces immediate action

Easier to control

Fail Silent Equipment

Degrades slowly

Creates hidden losses

Destroys margins quietly

Examples of silent failures:

Refrigeration running warm

Ice machines growing biofilm

Dish machines under-sanitizing

HVAC inefficiency driving energy cost

Rule:
Fail silent equipment must be monitored, logged, and verified — not trusted.

Repair Triage Protocol

(When Something Breaks)

When equipment fails, do not panic. Execute the triage.

Step 1: Classify the Failure

Is this Tier 1, 2, or 3?

Does service stop?

Is there a safety risk?

Step 2: Stabilize Service

Adjust menu if needed

Reassign stations

Communicate clearly to staff

Never troubleshoot emotionally during service.

Step 3: Decide Repair Path

Ask:

Can we operate safely tonight?

Is temporary mitigation acceptable?

Does this require emergency service?

Rule:
Emergency repairs are for service continuity, not convenience.

Step 4: Document Immediately

What failed?

When was last maintenance?

Was this preventable?

What changes reduce recurrence?

Undocumented failures repeat.

Vendor Control & Repair Discipline

(Vendors Work for You)

Approved Vendor List

Every restaurant must maintain:

Primary vendor

Secondary backup

Emergency contact

No random calls during service.

Repair Authorization Rules

Who can approve repairs?

Dollar limits per role?

When does owner approval trigger?

Undefined authority = overspending.

Post-Repair Review

After every repair:

Was this preventative failure?

Was vendor response acceptable?

Do we adjust maintenance cadence?

Repairs should improve the system, not just fix the problem.
