1. Core Principle (Don’t Skip This)

If it takes more than 2 minutes to use, owners won’t use it.
Your tool should feel like writing a recipe card, not accounting.

Every improvement below follows one rule:

Owners think in portions, not invoices.

⸻

2. Tool #1: New Plate Food Cost Calculator (Must-Have)

What It Should Do (Minimum Viable)

Inputs (fast, human-friendly):
	•	Menu item name
	•	Portion size (e.g., “6 oz chicken”)
	•	Ingredients list:
	•	Ingredient name
	•	Portion used (oz, each, tbsp, etc.)
	•	Cost per unit (auto-filled if known)

Outputs (instant):
	•	Total plate cost
	•	Food cost %
	•	Suggested menu price (optional margin presets)

Critical Improvements

✅ Ingredient Memory (Huge Win)
	•	Once “Chicken Breast – $2.85/lb” is entered, it’s reusable
	•	Auto-convert:
	•	lb → oz
	•	case → unit
	•	Let the owner update cost once, and all plates update

This is where most tools fail—and where yours wins.

⸻

✅ Smart Defaults (No Thinking Required)
	•	Default food cost targets:
	•	28% casual dining
	•	30% brunch
	•	32% seafood
	•	Default waste buffer:
	•	Proteins: +5%
	•	Produce: +10%

Make these editable but never blank.

⸻

✅ One-Click Reality Check
Add a button:

“Does this plate make sense?”

Output:
	•	Margin warning (green / yellow / red)
	•	“This item needs to sell for $X to hit 30% FC”

Owners love being told the truth fast.

⸻

3. Tool #2: Weekly / Monthly Food Cost Calculator

This is where most people overcomplicate it. Don’t.

What Owners Actually Want

Not:
	•	Inventory math
	•	COGS accounting
	•	Variance reports

They want:

“Did we make money on food this week?”

⸻

Simple Inputs (Non-Negotiable)

Option A (Fastest):
	•	Total food purchases (week or month)
	•	Total food sales

Output:
	•	Actual food cost %
	•	Target vs actual
	•	Dollar variance

That alone solves 80% of pain.

⸻

Power Upgrade (Still Simple)

Optional Inputs:
	•	Top 10 selling menu items (auto-pulled from POS later)
	•	Expected plate cost (from Tool #1)

Outputs:
	•	“Your food cost should have been X”
	•	“You’re off by $Y”
	•	Likely causes:
	•	Portion creep
	•	Waste
	•	Price increases
	•	Menu mix shift

No spreadsheets. No blame. Just signal.

⸻

4. UX Improvements That Matter More Than Features

🔹 Language Matters

Avoid:
	•	“COGS”
	•	“Variance”
	•	“Inventory depletion”

Use:
	•	“What you paid for food”
	•	“What food should have cost”
	•	“Where money leaked”

⸻

🔹 Mobile-First Inputs

Restaurant owners:
	•	Enter this on their phone
	•	Between tasks
	•	With greasy fingers

Large inputs. Big buttons. No tiny tables.

⸻

🔹 Save Everything Automatically

Never make them click “Save.”

If they leave:
	•	It’s saved
	•	They trust you more

⸻

5. Advanced (But Optional) Power Features

Only add these after the basics are loved:

🔸 POS Import (Later)
	•	Toast / Square / Clover
	•	Pull:
	•	Item sales counts
	•	Revenue totals

Match to plate costs automatically.

⸻

🔸 “What If” Pricing Mode

Slider:
	•	Raise menu price $1
	•	See margin impact instantly

This is addicting (in a good way).

⸻

🔸 Owner Coaching Output

At the bottom:

Owner Insight

	•	“Two items are dragging your food cost up.”
	•	“One protein increase erased $1,200 this month.”

This aligns perfectly with your Restaurant AI Consultant positioning.

⸻

6. What NOT to Build (Hard Truth)

❌ Full inventory systems
❌ Accounting exports first
❌ Recipe-level prep yield math early
❌ Anything that feels like QuickBooks

Those come later, if ever
