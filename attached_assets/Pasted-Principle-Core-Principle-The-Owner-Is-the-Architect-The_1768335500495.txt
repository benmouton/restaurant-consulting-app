Principle
Core Principle: The Owner Is the Architect

The owner’s job is to design and protect systems that operate without their constant presence — not to personally execute them.

Every hour the owner spends fixing problems that assigned leaders should handle is an hour stolen from:

building durable systems

developing leaders

increasing leverage and enterprise value

If the restaurant collapses when the owner steps away, the owner did not build a business — they built a dependency.

Framework
Role Clarity Framework (Non-Overlapping by Design)
OWNER (Architect & Capital Steward)

Owns:

Vision and cultural standards

Capital allocation and major financial decisions

Vendor relationships and contracts

Menu direction and pricing philosophy

Leadership selection and removal

Does NOT own:

Daily execution

Shift coverage

Real-time guest issues

Schedule mechanics

GENERAL MANAGER (Operational Authority)

Owns:

Daily operations and floor stability

Staff management, coaching, and discipline

Guest escalations beyond frontline recovery

Schedule approval and labor discipline

Enforcement of systems and standards

Escalates ONLY when:

Financial exposure exceeds defined thresholds

Legal or HR risk is present

Pattern failures indicate a broken system

KITCHEN MANAGER (Execution & Consistency)

Owns:

Food quality and plate consistency

Prep systems and pars

Line discipline and ticket flow

BOH training and hiring input

Immediate 86 decisions

Does NOT own:

Pricing decisions

Vendor changes

Menu expansion without approval

SHIFT LEAD (Real-Time Control)

Owns:

Floor control during service

Break execution and coverage

Immediate guest recovery within limits

Service pacing and table flow

Escalation to manager when thresholds are exceeded

Shift leads are control towers, not decision bottlenecks.

Checklist
Decision Rights Matrix (Written Authority Prevents Chaos)

WHO DECIDES WHAT:

□ Comp under $25 → Server (manager notified)
□ Comp $25–$100 → Manager only
□ Comp over $100 → Owner approval
□ Staff sent home early → Shift lead
□ Staff termination → GM + Owner
□ Menu item 86 → KM immediately, FOH notified
□ Equipment repair under $500 → GM
□ Vendor change → Owner

Rule: If authority is not written, it does not exist.

Framework
What the Owner Must Stop Carrying
STOP CARRYING:

Individual staff scheduling conflicts

Minor guest complaints that managers can resolve

Daily prep and line decisions

Register reconciliation and close-out

Opening or closing shifts out of habit

Carrying these trains the organization to wait for you.

START DELEGATING — WITH STRUCTURE:

Clear expectations (written, not implied)

Defined authority boundaries

Verification cadence (not trust-only)

Documented consequences for failure

Public recognition for success

Delegation without verification is abdication.
Verification without trust is micromanagement.
The goal is measured autonomy.

Control Loop (What Makes This Work)

Every delegated responsibility must include:

Owner — who is accountable

Authority — what decisions they can make

Standard — what “right” looks like

Verification — how it’s checked

Correction — what happens when it fails

If a task keeps coming back to the owner, the failure is not the person — it’s the system.

Consultant’s Bottom Line

The owner’s highest value work is not solving today’s problems.
It is designing systems that prevent tomorrow’s problems.

If you are still the safety net, you are not free — and neither is your business.
