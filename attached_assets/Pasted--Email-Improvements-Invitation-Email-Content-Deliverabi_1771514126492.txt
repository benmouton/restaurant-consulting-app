## 2. Email Deliverability — Preventing Junk/Spam Folder

The email is being flagged for two reasons shown in the screenshot:
1. **"Newly Registered Domain"** — your domain restaurantai.consulting is new and hasn't built sender reputation yet
2. **"Mail thinks this message is Junk Mail"** — the content and/or sending configuration is triggering spam filters

### Fix these in order of impact:

### A. Set up email authentication records (CRITICAL — do this first)

Add these DNS records for your domain. Check your domain registrar or wherever restaurantai.consulting DNS is managed:

**SPF Record:**
- Type: TXT
- Host: @
- Value: `v=spf1 include:[your-email-service-spf] ~all`
- (Replace with the SPF include for whatever service sends your emails — SendGrid, Resend, AWS SES, Postmark, etc.)

**DKIM Record:**
- Your email service provider will give you a DKIM key to add as a TXT or CNAME record
- This cryptographically signs your emails so recipients know they're really from you

**DMARC Record:**
- Type: TXT
- Host: _dmarc
- Value: `v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@restaurantai.consulting`
- Start with `p=none` (monitoring mode), then move to `p=quarantine` once you confirm everything works

**If you're not sure which email service you're using**, search your codebase for email sending logic — look for packages like `@sendgrid/mail`, `resend`, `nodemailer`, `postmark`, `aws-ses`, or similar. Then check that service's documentation for DNS setup instructions.

### B. Set up a proper "From" address

**Current:** `noreply@restaurantai.consulting`

**Problems:**
- The domain still says "restaurantai" — update to match the rebrand if possible
- `noreply@` addresses score poorly with spam filters
- Consider using `ben@therestaurantconsultant.com` or `team@therestaurantconsultant.com` instead

**If you can register a new domain** (e.g., `therestaurantconsultant.com`):
- Set up all DNS authentication records on the new domain
- Use that domain for sending emails
- This also fixes the "newly registered domain" issue if the new domain has some age, or at least gives you a clean start with proper authentication

**If staying on the current domain:**
- At minimum, change from `noreply@` to a real-sounding address like `ben@restaurantai.consulting` or `team@restaurantai.consulting`
- Set up a reply-to address so recipients can actually respond

### C. Warm up the sending domain

New domains that suddenly start sending dozens of emails get flagged. Warm up gradually:

- **Week 1:** Send to 5-10 people per day maximum
- **Week 2:** Increase to 20-30 per day
- **Week 3:** Increase to 50+ per day
- Ask early recipients to **open the email, click the link, and reply** — this trains email providers that your emails are wanted
- Ask recipients to move the email from Junk to Inbox if it lands there — this directly improves your sender reputation with that email provider

### D. Content changes that reduce spam scoring

Spam filters score emails on content patterns. Fix these:

- **Remove "exclusive access"** — spam trigger phrase
- **Don't use ALL CAPS** in any part of the email (the current "CONSULTANT" in the header)
- **Reduce the number of links** — keep it to one primary CTA button ("Get Started") and minimal footer links
- **Include a plain text version** of the email alongside the HTML version — emails with only HTML score higher on spam filters
- **Include a physical mailing address** in the footer — required by CAN-SPAM law and improves deliverability
- **Include an unsubscribe link** in the footer — also required by law and improves spam scoring
- **Keep the email short** — your current length is good, don't add more

### E. Add these to the email footer for compliance and deliverability:

```
The Restaurant Consultant

[Your business address]

```

### F. Consider switching email providers if needed

Some providers have better deliverability than others for transactional emails:

**Best for deliverability:**
- **Postmark** — specifically designed for transactional emails, excellent deliverability
- **Resend** — modern, developer-friendly, good deliverability
- **AWS SES** — cheap and reliable once warmed up, but requires more setup

**Avoid for transactional emails:**
- Generic SMTP through your hosting provider
- Gmail SMTP (rate limits and deliverability issues)

## 3. Consider Splitting Invitation from Marketing Emails

The invitation email is a **transactional email** (someone took an action that triggered it), not a marketing email. Transactional emails should:
- Come from a different subdomain or sender than marketing emails (e.g., `notifications@` vs `marketing@`)
- Be sent through a transactional email service, not a marketing email service
- Have minimal design — simple, clean, text-focused
- Not include marketing content or promotional language

The current email is already mostly transactional in tone, which is good. Just remove the "exclusive" language and keep it straightforward: "You were invited. Here's what you get. Click to start."
