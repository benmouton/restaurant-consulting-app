1) Fix the biggest problem first: your inputs are too “vibe-based.”

Across Readiness, Alerts, Debrief, and Coaching, you rely almost entirely on free-text boxes. That forces your AI to hedge (“assume…”, “unknown status…”) and makes your readiness score feel soft.

Replace most big text boxes with fast structured inputs (clicks > typing)

READINESS (pre-service) — make this a 45–60 second checklist:

A. Prep
	•	Prep signed off? → Yes / No
	•	Time signed off → time picker
	•	Station status (tap to set):
	•	Sauté / Grill / Fry / Pantry / Expo → 🟢 🟡 🔴
	•	Top 3 at-risk items → multi-select from your actual prep list

B. Pars / 86 Risk
	•	Any par shortages? → None / Minor / Severe
	•	86 list → multi-select
	•	“Next 60 min risk” → Low / Medium / High

C. Staffing
	•	Headcount vs plan → –2 / –1 / On plan / +1 / +2
	•	Expo strength → Weak / OK / Strong
	•	Weakest station today → dropdown

D. Volume
	•	Forecasted covers → number
	•	Large parties? → Yes / No (size?)
	•	To-go load → Low / Med / High

👉 Keep one small notes box at the bottom — everything else should be buttons.

⸻

2) Make the Readiness Score explainable (so it’s actually trusted)

Right now: “85/100” feels arbitrary.

Instead, show this breakdown on screen:

Kitchen Readiness Score: 85/100
	•	Prep readiness: 24/30
	•	Pars & 86 risk: 15/20
	•	Staffing coverage: 16/20
	•	Ticket-flow risk: 18/20
	•	Line set & sanitation: 12/10

Then — and this is the key part — show:

Top 3 drivers that lowered the score
	1.	Sauté marked 🟡
	2.	Prep sign-off missing
	3.	Large party at 7pm

Top 3 fixes (+ point impact)
	•	Move senior cook to sauté (+6)
	•	Verify backup pars on proteins (+4)
	•	Confirm expo runner coverage (+3)

Now your AI is a coach, not a poet.

⸻

3) Turn “Alerts” into real-time decision support

Your Alerts tab is still free text. Convert it into “if/then” style triggers.

Collect:
	•	Avg app time (mins)
	•	Avg entrée time (mins)
	•	Window holding food? → Yes / No
	•	Current cover pace → Low / Medium / High
	•	Bottleneck station → dropdown

Then have the AI output something like:

Service Alerts (Now)
	•	Risk: Grill backing up
	•	Action: Shift fry cook to grill support
	•	Trigger: If entrées exceed 22 min for 10 minutes
	•	86 risk: Shrimp — push chicken/sausage as default upsell

This is what separates a toy from a tool.

⸻

4) Make the Quick Debrief actually feed tomorrow

Right now it’s three nice boxes — but they disappear into the void.

Add a simple structure behind it:

After “Log Debrief”:
	•	Store:
	•	Bottleneck: (expo / sauté / grill / fry / prep / runners)
	•	Root cause: (prep short / staffing / comms / equipment / menu)
	•	One fix: (dropdown + owner + due time)

Then — on the NEXT day’s Readiness screen — surface this:

Carryover from last shift
	•	“Yesterday: sauté ran out of compound butter (Owner: Prep, Due: 3:45pm)”

That creates real accountability.

⸻

5) Make “Coaching” sharper — one behavior, one fix

Your Coaching tab is good in spirit, but still too open.

Change it to:

Pick ONE to coach today:
	•	Timing discipline
	•	Callouts / heard backs
	•	Station organization
	•	Prep ownership
	•	Expo communication

Then AI outputs:
	•	30-second talk track
	•	One observable standard (“I expect two ‘heard’ confirmations per ticket”)
	•	One drill for that station

This keeps managers from trying to fix everything at once.

⸻

6) Your Presets are good — make them smarter

Your preset list is strong. Make each one auto-fill risks:

Example — Busy Friday Dinner auto-loads:
	•	Covers: 140–180
	•	Staffing: On plan
	•	Sauté → 🟡 by default
	•	“Next 60 min risk” → Medium
	•	Suggested plan pre-written

You’re basically building a library of battle plans.
