Here’s how a top 1% restaurant operator would look at this scheduling interface and immediately want to upgrade it — because right now it’s functional but very manual, error-prone, and disconnected from the real pain points of running a restaurant floor.
Current reality check (what actually hurts operators)

You have to add every single shift manually → very tedious
No visibility into labor cost %, coverage gaps, or over/under staffing
No forecasting or smart suggestions — it’s just a blank calendar
No connection to sales projections, reservations, historical patterns, or staff availability
Adding staff is one-by-one → no bulk import
No quick way to copy a good week forward
No alerts for common mistakes (double-booking, missing breaks, overtime risk)

This is basically a digital paper calendar — not a scheduling engine.

Suggested evolution path (realistic versions)
Version 1 – Make it feel alive (1–4 weeks work)

Add labor % & $ forecast at top of each day/week
(even if it’s just “Projected $1,820 | 31.4%” — users will love seeing the number)
Color-code days: green = good coverage, yellow = thin, red = major gap
Add “Copy this week → next week” button
Add simple position presets dropdown when adding shift
(Server, Bartender, Host, Line Cook, Dish, Expo, etc.)

Version 2 – Reduce typing dramatically (next 1–2 months)

“Suggest staffing” button per day
→ “Based on last 4 Mondays: 4 servers, 1 bartender, 2 BOH”
Drag-and-drop shifts + duplicate shift button
Staff list with availability calendar view (staff can update via mobile)
Quick-add multiple shifts (add 4 servers at once for dinner)

Version 3 – Smart & connected (3–6 months)

Pull projected covers from reservations/POS (or manual input once per week)
Show “You need X more covers to justify this labor” or “Safe to cut 1 server after 9 pm”
Auto-warnings: “Exceeds labor budget by $220”, “Missing expo 6–10 pm”
Publish & notify: one button → texts/emails schedule to everyone

Visual & UX upgrades operators love

Big number at top: “This week labor forecast: $9,420 | 29.8%”
Coverage bars under each day (green = full, yellow = thin, red = holes)
Staff avatars/icons on shifts (makes it feel like real people)
Mobile-optimized week view (operators check schedule on phone constantly)
“Unpublished changes” banner — “You have 6 unsaved shifts — publish now?”
