# Upgrade Team Invitation — Custom Email with Personal Touch

The current "Invite Team Member" flow sends a generic invitation. We need to upgrade it so the invite email feels personal, warm, and makes the recipient feel like a valued early tester — not just another user.

---

## 1. Update the Invite Form

**File:** Wherever the "Invite Team Member" button/modal lives (likely in the profile/account settings page)

### Add new fields to the invite form:
- **Email address** (already exists)
- **Their first name** (new field — so the email can address them personally)
- **Their role/relationship** (dropdown: Fellow Operator, Manager, Industry Friend, Mentor/Advisor, Other)
- **Personal message** (optional textarea, pre-filled with a default they can edit):

Default message:
```
Hey [first name],

I've been building something I'm really excited about — a restaurant operations platform called The Restaurant Consultant. It's built from everything I've learned running my own restaurants, and I'd love your honest feedback on it.

You know this business better than most, and your perspective would mean a lot to me as I shape this into something that actually helps operators like us.

Take a look when you get a chance — no pressure, no sales pitch. Just want to know if this is something you'd actually use on a Tuesday night when everything's going sideways.

— Ben
```

- The personal message should be fully editable before sending
- Pre-fill "[first name]" with whatever they entered in the name field
- Pre-fill the sign-off with the current user's first name

---

## 2. Build the Custom Email Template

### Email design:
- Clean, simple design — NOT a heavy marketing email with banners and graphics
- Should feel like a personal email, not an automated blast
- Use the app's dark color scheme subtly (dark header bar with logo, otherwise clean white/light email body for readability in email clients)

### Email structure:

```
─────────────────────────────────────────
[Chef Hat Logo]  The Restaurant Consultant
─────────────────────────────────────────

Hey [first name],

[Personal message from the invite form — whatever Ben wrote or the default]

────────────────

Here's what you'll get access to:

🔪 12 operational domains — from kitchen readiness to HR compliance
📋 Training templates and employee handbooks
💰 Food cost tools and financial analysis
🚨 Crisis management playbooks
💬 An operations consultant for any question

────────────────

[  Accept Invitation  →  ]   (big, clear CTA button)

This invitation expires in 7 days.

────────────────

Questions? Just reply to this email — it goes directly to [Ben's email].

─────────────────────────────────────────
The Restaurant Consultant
Systems that work on your worst night.
─────────────────────────────────────────
```

### Key email details:
- **From name:** "[Ben Mouton] via The Restaurant Consultant" (personal, not corporate)
- **Reply-to:** Set to Ben's actual email address so replies go to him, not a no-reply address
- **Subject line options** (let the inviter pick or use a smart default):
  - Default: "Hey [first name] — I'd love your feedback on something I'm building"
  - Alternative: "[Ben] invited you to try The Restaurant Consultant"
  - Alternative: "Got 10 minutes? I built something for operators like us"
- The subject should feel personal, not automated. Default to the first option.

---

## 3. Post-Send Experience

### After the invite is sent:
- Show a success message: "Invitation sent to [name] at [email]! They have 7 days to accept."
- Show the sent email preview so Ben can see exactly what was sent
- Add a "Send another" button for quick batch inviting

### Track invite status:
- In the Pending Invitations list on the Account Settings page, show:
  - Recipient name (not just email)
  - Date sent
  - Status: Sent → Opened → Accepted (if email tracking is possible) or just Sent → Accepted/Expired
  - Expiration date
  - "Resend" button for expired invitations (resend with a fresh link and updated expiration)

---

## 4. Acceptance Landing Page

### When the recipient clicks "Accept Invitation":
- Don't just dump them on a generic signup page
- Show a personalized welcome:
  - "Hey [first name], [Ben] invited you to check out The Restaurant Consultant."
  - "[Ben]'s message: [personal message preview]"
  - Simple signup form: Name, email (pre-filled), password
  - "Create Account & Start Exploring" button
- After account creation, take them directly to the dashboard with a first-time user experience
- Consider giving invited users a **free trial period** (7 or 14 days) so they can actually explore before being asked to subscribe — this is critical for getting real feedback

---

## 5. Follow-Up Reminder (Optional but Valuable)

### If the invitation hasn't been accepted after 3 days:
- Auto-send a gentle reminder email:

```
Subject: Quick follow-up — still want your take on this

Hey [first name],

Just bumping this — I sent you access to The Restaurant Consultant a few days ago. No rush at all, but if you get 10 minutes, I'd really appreciate your honest take.

Here's your invite link: [Accept Invitation →]

Expires in [X] days.

— Ben
```

- Only send ONE reminder, not a drip campaign — respect their time
- Let Ben opt out of auto-reminders per invitation if he wants

---

## Priority Order
1. **Custom email template with personal message** (the core feature)
2. **Updated invite form with name and editable message** (enables personalization)
3. **Personalized acceptance landing page** (better first impression)
4. **Invite status tracking** (visibility into what's working)
5. **Auto-reminder at 3 days** (increases acceptance rate without being pushy)
